COVID-19 Policy

Public Health Guidelines

  • If you have possible COVID-19 symptoms do not enter the venue.
  • If you are unwell or have COVID-19 symptoms, stay at home.
  • Call Healthline on 0800 358 5453 or contact your general practice if you are experiencing any COVID-19 symptoms.
  • Take a RAT test if you suspect you may have COVID-19.

Good Health, Hygiene and Safety Practices

  • Maintain basic hygiene measures, such as frequent hand washing and mask wearing.
  • Sneeze or cough into your elbow and avoid touching your face. This will prevent the risk of spreading COVID-19, and other viruses such as the common cold or flu.
  • Facilities are available so you can regularly wash and dry your hands.
  • Hand sanitiser is available throughout the venue.
  • Masks are provided for all public and volunteers to use indoors and where physical distancing from others cannot be maintained.
  • Avoid sharing personal objects. If this is not possible, sanitize hands before and after use and clean objects between use as often as possible.

Use of Masks and Face Coverings

  • Face coverings at our events are always encouraged. Face coverings are a way we can protect ourselves and each other.
  • Masks can be removed for meal, refreshment, and comfort breaks, however physical distancing should be maintained at these times.
  • Try not to speak, shout or sing close to others without a mask.

Face Mask Exemptions

  • We encourage volunteers and the public who must wear face coverings to do so.
  • It’s also important to remember that for some people a face covering is unsuitable because of a disability or health condition.
  • Some people will have an exemption card with them to show that there is a reason why a face covering is unsuitable for them.
  • Exemption cards are not mandatory, so some people will not have one with them.
  • Be kind and respectful of privacy when approaching someone who isn’t wearing a face covering.
  • While it’s not always obvious why a face covering is unsuitable, it’s inappropriate to inquire about the nature of a person’s disability or condition.
  • Face coverings are just one of the tools we have for helping to stop the spread of COVID-19.
  • When a face covering is unsuitable, it is always important to enable other good health, hygiene and safety practices.
